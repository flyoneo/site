About Me

I'm a motivated Junior studying computer science at UC Berkeley, and I'm seeking an internship over Summer 2022. As a third-year, I have taken a wide variety of courses: Computer Security, Algorithms, Data Structures, Computer Architecture, Intro to AI, and Operating Systems. Yet, just like in class, internships at big companies deal with ‘concepts’ and not actual ‘doing’. Because I find the greatest motivation when I see immediate results of my work; so, I’ve flung myself into entrepreneurial settings. Last summer at Berkeley Lab, I enjoyed applying midterm studies to predict actual water temperature in continental streams across the US. I designed and built a voice-controlled robot car from op-amps to PCA. When not in classes, I write scrapers and scripts to generate a dataset (see TMTrack) or simply help classmates mass download several chapters. Companies are not only looking for raw skill but also for individuals that transform the world in code — I am confident that I can contribute meaningfully as an intern.

Coming from an immigrant background, I got little exposure to tech. My friends told me that CS 61A would be too difficult for me — I proved them wrong. It was my fellow classmates who helped me become the confident problem-solver I am today. From struggling to write a single line of code in CS 61A, I became a lab assistant in CS 61A. I’ve been trying to give back by helping students from diverse backgrounds. I hope to share high-fives and learn our languages as code tests pass at work too!

Fast forward to now, my team at Verizon and I became finalists at the Global Venture Catalyst competition — we tackled discrimination in hiring. I also was fortunate to work with an early-career researcher at Berkeley Lab. Managing a large codebase of scientific code and investigating the new field of applied machine learning to climate change, I was also able to co-author my first paper in Water journal! It felt like I was combining my knowledge from classes and my inner hobbyist all in one!It is hard to convey passion through a piece of paper, but what differentiates me from other applicants is my curiosity to make things better in the most lazy way possible. Building productivity tools to track my time spent (because no other productivity tool fits) and spending 30 mins to make a dataset generator for a personal project instead of using one from Kaggle (and ending up with a Kaggle-publishable dataset), these are the qualities I am most proud of and which I most enjoy applying to my work.

I’d love to share more about my experiences. If there’s anything you’d like to know, feel free to reach out! Thanks for checking me out!
